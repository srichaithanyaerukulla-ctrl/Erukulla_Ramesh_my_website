FAQ

Common Questions Answered

Everything you want to know about LIC, insurance and investing — answered simply.

Is LIC safe? Can the government close it?+
Yes — LIC of India is 100% safe. It is backed by the full faith and sovereign guarantee of the Government of India under the Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956. The government cannot close LIC. Your money is constitutionally protected.
How much tax can I save under Section 80C?+
Under Section 80C, you can claim a deduction of up to ₹1,50,000 per year on LIC premiums. For a person in the 30% tax bracket, this means you save up to ₹46,800 in taxes every year — just from your LIC premium.
Is SIP better than an LIC endowment plan?+
They serve different purposes. SIP (Mutual Funds) offers higher potential returns but with market risk — ideal for long-term wealth building. LIC endowment plans offer guaranteed returns, life cover AND tax benefits — ideal for safety-first families. I recommend both — one for protection, one for growth.
What documents are needed to file a LIC claim?+
For a death claim: Original policy bond, death certificate, nominee's ID proof, cancelled cheque. For maturity: Original policy bond, ID proof, cancelled cheque. I personally help all my clients with claim documentation — free of charge, throughout the entire process.
What is the minimum age to buy a LIC policy?+
Most LIC plans can be taken from age 18. Child plans allow entry from day 1 (birth) or 30 days old. The earlier you start, the lower the premium and the higher the benefit. Contact me to find the best plan for any age.
Is the maturity amount from LIC fully tax-free?+
Yes! Under Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act, the maturity amount from LIC policies is fully tax-free — provided your annual premium is less than 10% of the sum assured. This makes LIC one of the best tax-free wealth building instruments in India.
What is the difference between Jeevan Umang and Jeevan Utsav?+
Jeevan Umang: You pay premium for 15–30 years, then receive annual income for life (till age 99). Jeevan Utsav: You pay a single one-time premium, and receive ₹1 Lakh per year after 7 years — for lifetime. Both give lifetime income, but Utsav is for those with a lump sum to invest at once.
Can I get a loan against my LIC policy?+
Yes. After your policy acquires a surrender value (typically after 3 years of premiums), you can take a loan of up to 90% of the surrender value. The interest rate is low, and your policy continues to earn bonus. This is one of LIC's most powerful features for emergency liquidity.
What is Bima Kavach and who should buy it?+
Bima Kavach (Plan 887) is LIC's newest pure term plan launched in December 2025. It offers coverage up to age 100 with a minimum sum assured of ₹2 Crore. It is ideal for high-income earners, business owners, and anyone who needs very high life cover with life-stage enhancement options. It can only be purchased offline through a licensed agent like me.
How do I start a SIP mutual fund investment?+
Starting a SIP is very simple: Choose your goal (retirement, child education, house), choose the monthly amount (as low as ₹500), choose a fund category (equity/debt/hybrid), complete KYC once with your Aadhaar and PAN, and your monthly SIP starts automatically.
